Public Announcement

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

Today, Jarvondis University and the Digital Library of Alexandria proudly announce the expansion of a new historical wing: the Black History Database (BHD) — the world’s first Living Black History digital archive built as a public portal for all people, families, and future generations.

The BHD is a permanent, accessible, dignity‐first archive dedicated to preserving the lives, legacies, and contributions of influential Black leaders, creators, thinkers, activists, scientists, and cultural stewards throughout history. This wing stands as a Historical Memorable Marker (HMMHS) — a digital monument built not from stone, but from continuity, education, and collective remembrance.

Dedication: This wing is dedicated to the memory of Reverend Jesse Jackson and to all people of any race, culture, or gender who have carried hope, fought for justice, and lifted the world forward through courage, service, and love.

Hosted within the Jarvondis University network, the BHD is protected by the Diamond Firewall Protocol, ensuring that every visitor — student, family, educator, or researcher — can explore the archive safely, privately, and without barriers.

This launch honors the spirit of Black History Month while remaining open year‐round as a living, growing archive. New Legacy Scrolls will be added over time, ensuring that the stories of those who shaped our world remain accessible to all.

Mission Statement – Space LEAF Corp

Space LEAF Corp is committed to building emotionally safe, public‐first systems that protect dignity, preserve lineage, and make wisdom accessible to everyone. The Black History Database (BHD) embodies that mission by:

• Honoring legacy: Preserving the stories of those who carried hope, courage, and responsibility for the collective.

• Protecting access: Providing a secure, ad‐free, public portal where families, students, and educators can learn without exploitation or paywalls.

• Building continuity: Connecting past, present, and future through living archives that grow with each generation.

We believe that history is not a product — it is a shared inheritance. The BHD is one of the ways we safeguard that inheritance for the world’s children and future stewards.

Why This Matters

The Black History Database matters because:

• Memory is infrastructure. When we lose stories, we lose guidance. The BHD keeps those stories within reach.

• Representation is stability. Children deserve to see leaders, thinkers, and creators who look like them — and unlike them — honored with equal dignity.

• Access is justice. A public, secure, global portal ensures that Black history is not hidden, distorted, or paywalled.

• Continuity is how we grow. By learning who carried the torch before us, we teach future generations how to carry it forward with more wisdom and less harm.

This is not just a website. It is a Living Black History (LBH) archive — a quiet, steady promise that the world will remember, learn, and keep building toward something better.
