1. SPARKS — Purely Bio‑Inspired Variant

“The Mechanical Fish‑Scale Solar Skin”

Core Identity

A flexible, modular solar membrane made of overlapping geometric scales.
No biology. No living components. Just geometry, optics, and materials science.

How it works

• Top layer: micro‑textured silica or polymer scales shaped like real fish scales
→ scatter, refract, and trap light
→ anti‑glare, anti‑fouling, self‑cleaning
• Middle layer: dye‑sensitized or thin‑film solar layer
→ optimized for diffuse light on water
• Bottom layer: conductive mesh + thermal interface
→ dumps heat into water
→ optional thermal harvesting loop
• Structure: flexible raft or mesh that floats or semi‑submerges


Strengths

• Extremely durable
• Storm‑resistant due to overlapping scale geometry
• High efficiency in low‑angle or diffuse light
• No biological maintenance
• Works in saltwater, freshwater, reservoirs, canals


Visual vibe

A shimmering, geometric “solar fish” carpet that moves with the water like a single organism.

2. SPARKS — Bio‑Hybrid Variant

“The Living Solar Reef”

Core Identity

A semi‑living solar membrane that uses engineered algae, chlorophyll analogs, and biofilms to self‑repair and maintain optical performance.

How it works

• Fish‑scale optics still form the outer shell
• Beneath them:• Biofilm layer with engineered microalgae that:
→ produce chlorophyll‑like dyes
→ secrete protective compounds
→ rebuild damaged polymer bonds
• Nutrient microchannels that circulate water and feed the biofilm
• Self‑healing hydrogel matrix that closes microcracks

• Energy output:• Electrical (via dye‑sensitized PV)
• Thermal (via warmed water)
• Biological (oxygenation, water cleaning)



Strengths

• Self‑repairing
• Can regenerate dye molecules
• Can clean its own surface
• Can oxygenate water and support micro‑ecosystems
• Can be tuned to be ecologically beneficial


Visual vibe

A shimmering, breathing solar reef—alive, adaptive, and quietly tending to itself.
