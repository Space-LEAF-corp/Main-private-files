Remote Deep‑Space Resource Mission Protocol (RDSRMP)

A concise operational standard for safe, ethical, non‑contact extraterrestrial material recovery.

---

1. Mission Purpose

Enable the safe, robotic retrieval of non‑biological deep‑space materials for research and sustainable resource use, while ensuring zero risk to human personnel and zero disturbance to any environment that may contain life.

---

2. Core Ethical Directives

2.1 Robots First

All initial detection, scanning, sampling, extraction, and containment operations must be performed by remote or autonomous robotic systems.
Humans remain in supervisory roles only.

2.2 No Contact With Life

If any sign of biological activity is detected — chemical, geometric, atmospheric, or behavioral — the site becomes strictly non‑interference.
No sampling, extraction, or disturbance is permitted.

2.3 Non‑Biological Materials Only

Only materials confirmed to be non‑living and non‑biological may be extracted.
Priority targets include:

• Ice
• Metals
• Volatiles
• Mineral composites
• Non‑reactive debris


---

3. Pre‑Contact Safety Requirements

3.1 Multi‑Layer Hazard Scan

Before any physical interaction, the robotic system must complete a full Pre‑Contact Hazard Scan (PCHS) including:

• Chemical composition
• Vapor/dust atmospheric signature
• Radiological profile
• Nano‑particle distribution
• EM/field interaction
• Energetic/metastable potential
• Cryochemical future‑state behavior
• Geometry vs natural formation patterns
• Unknown/exotic anomaly detection


3.2 Safety Tier Assignment

Based on PCHS results, the target is assigned a Safety Tier:

• Tier 0: Natural, low hazard
• Tier 1: Mild anomaly or single‑dimension hazard
• Tier 2: Multi‑dimension hazard or high anomaly
• Tier 3: Unknown‑critical — no contact permitted


Only Tier 0–2 may be extracted.

---

4. Extraction Protocol

4.1 Cryogenic Integrity Handling

All extraction must preserve the target’s temperature and phase state.
No heating, melting, or mechanical stress that could trigger reactions.

4.2 Non‑Contact Isolation

Use:

• Cryogenic coring
• Remote fracturing
• Magnetic or electrostatic capture
• Containment pods


No direct tool‑to‑material contact unless hazard logic approves.

4.3 Sealed Return Containment

All recovered material must be stored in:

• Multi‑layer sealed pods
• Temperature‑controlled chambers
• Hazard‑tagged containers


No material enters a crewed environment until cleared.

---

5. Return & Integration

5.1 Quarantine

All returned samples undergo:

• Chemical re‑analysis
• Radiological screening
• Nano‑particle containment checks
• EM interference testing


5.2 Approval for Use

Only after full clearance may materials enter:

• Research labs
• Manufacturing pipelines
• Resource processing systems


---

6. Mission Termination Conditions

The mission must halt extraction and retreat if:

• Any sign of life is detected
• Hazard classification escalates to Tier 3
• Containment integrity is compromised
• Unknown or exotic signatures exceed safe thresholds


---

7. Guiding Principle

“No human contact with uncharacterized matter. No interaction with any environment showing signs of life. All initial operations are robotic, fully contained, and aimed at non‑biological resource recovery to reduce extraction from Earth.”
