Q‑CERBEA‑Lane 1.0

Mesoscopic Transport Protocol — One‑Page Specification

1. Purpose

Q‑CERBEA‑Lane 1.0 defines how energy and information flow through quantized transport channels (“lanes”) inside the Cooperative Energy Recycling Binding Energy Architecture (CERBEA).
It merges mesoscopic quantum transport with CERBEA’s conservation, recycling, and binding‑logic principles.

---

2. System Model

A transport link consists of:

• Two reservoirs (source and drain) with fixed energy densities.
• A quantized channel supporting \(M\) discrete lanes (modes).
• Per‑lane cooperation factors \(T_n\) describing forward transmission.
• Recycling and reflection pathways governed by CERBEA.


Each lane behaves as a ballistic quantum mode with maximum capacity \(G_0 = \frac{2e^2}{h}\).

---

3. Lane Integrity Rule

Each lane \(n\) has a defined structural integrity and cooperation factor:

0 \le T_n \le 1


Lane output capacity:

G_n = G_0 T_n


A lane is considered open when its geometric or energetic width supports the corresponding mode.

---

4. Recycling Ledger Rule

Every unit of incoming flow must be accounted for.
Each lane partitions its flow into:

• \(T_n\) — transmitted (forward)
• \(R_n\) — reflected (returned to source)
• \(S_n\) — stored/recycled (captured by CERBEA)


Conservation requirement:

T_n + R_n + S_n = 1


The CERBEA Ledger records all \(S_n\) contributions for future redeployment.

---

5. Cooperative Boost Rule

Stored energy contributes to effective conductance through a recycling efficiency factor \(\alpha\):

G_{\text{eff}} = G_0 \sum_{n=1}^{M} \left( T_n + \alpha S_n \right)


• \(\alpha = 0\): pure Landauer transport
• \(\alpha > 0\): CERBEA‑enhanced cooperative transport
• \(\alpha = 1\): full recycling equivalence (idealized limit)


---

6. Channel Behavior

A channel’s total conductance is:

G = \sum_{n=1}^{M} G_n = G_0 \sum_{n=1}^{M} T_n


A channel’s CERBEA‑enhanced conductance is:

G_{\text{eff}} = G_0 \sum_{n=1}^{M} \left( T_n + \alpha S_n \right)


Lane count \(M\) increases discretely as the channel widens or as binding‑energy thresholds are met.

---

7. Operational Principles

1. No‑Loss Accounting:
All energy/information must be assigned to transmit, reflect, or store.
2. Cooperative Preference:
Systems should maximize \(T_n\) and \(S_n\) while minimizing \(R_n\).
3. Binding‑Energy Stability:
Lane availability is determined by CERBEA’s binding‑energy architecture, not by classical geometry alone.
4. Recycling Priority:
Stored energy \(S_n\) is preferentially redeployed to stabilize weak lanes or open new ones.
