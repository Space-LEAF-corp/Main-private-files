1.0 Mission Purpose

The Lunar Convoy System enables safe, mobile habitation on the lunar surface using a coordinated fleet consisting of:

• Command Habitat RV
• Lunar Hopper Space Car
• Orbital Roof Observation Dome
• Anchor & Stabilization Array
• Deployable Camping Garage HQ


The system replaces fixed lunar bases with mobile, resilient, human‑centered exploration units capable of relocating, anchoring, and sheltering during lunar storms.

---

2.0 System Overview

2.1 Command Habitat RV (“The Home”)

Primary living, navigation, and safety module.

Capabilities:

• Pressurized cabin with full life‑support redundancy
• Orbital Roof for safe cosmic viewing
• Dual airlocks
• Rear docking spine for garage deployment
• Integrated anchor hardpoints
• Radiation‑shielded hull


Operational Role:
Acts as the convoy’s core, providing shelter, command, medical support, and long‑range mobility.

---

2.2 Lunar Hopper Space Car (“The Scout”)

Short‑range exploration vehicle.

Capabilities:

• Pressurized micro‑cabin
• High‑torque electric motors
• Magnetic/harpoon micro‑anchors
• Quick‑dock compatibility with RV and garage
• Emergency tether return system


Operational Role:
Performs scouting, sampling, short‑range travel, and emergency retrieval.

---

2.3 Orbital Roof (“The Cosmic Window”)

Reinforced transparent dome for safe observation.

Capabilities:

• Radiation‑filtered composite
• Micrometeorite‑resistant outer shell
• Internal shutters for solar events


Operational Role:
Provides psychological comfort, navigation visibility, and educational observation for crew and children.

---

2.4 Anchor & Stabilization Array

Multi‑mode anchoring system.

Capabilities:

• Drill anchors for regolith
• Harpoon anchors for rock
• Electrostatic adhesion pads
• Cross‑brace tension lines
• Storm‑lock mode


Operational Role:
Secures the RV and garage during storms, quakes, or docking operations.

---

2.5 Deployable Camping Garage HQ

Temporary shelter and workspace.

Capabilities:

• Fold‑out carbon‑titanium frame
• Inflatable rigidized walls
• Regolith‑throw shielding
• Docking cradle for space car
• Antenna mast
• Tool racks and workbench


Operational Role:
Acts as a temporary HQ, storm shelter for the space car, and field workshop.

3.0 Convoy Operations

3.1 Startup Sequence

1. Initialize RV systems
2. Verify life‑support redundancy
3. Activate navigation suite
4. Deploy external sensors
5. Run hull integrity scan
6. Confirm anchor array readiness
7. Power up space car (standby mode)


---

3.2 Transit Mode

Used when the convoy is moving across the lunar surface.

Checklist:

• Retract all anchors
• Fold and secure garage
• Lock docking spine
• Confirm space car is secured
• Set suspension to “low‑gravity terrain mode”
• Maintain safe speed bands


Crew Protocol:

• Remain strapped in during rough terrain
• Use Orbital Roof shutters during high‑sun exposure
• Maintain comms with Hopper if deployed separately


---

3.3 Landing & Anchoring Procedure

1. Select stable terrain
2. Deploy drill anchors
3. Deploy harpoon anchors if rocky
4. Engage electrostatic adhesion
5. Cross‑brace tension lines
6. Switch to Storm‑Lock Mode if needed


Confirmation:
All anchor indicators must show green before garage deployment.

---

3.4 Garage Deployment Sequence

1. Unlock rear docking spine
2. Extend carbon‑titanium frame
3. Inflate rigidized walls
4. Deploy regolith‑throw shielding
5. Anchor garage perimeter
6. Activate internal lighting and tool racks
7. Dock space car into cradle


HQ Mode Activation:

• Raise antenna mast
• Connect power umbilicals
• Enable external workbench systems


---

3.5 Storm Protocol

Triggered during dust storms, solar events, or micrometeorite alerts.

RV Actions:

• Engage Storm‑Lock anchors
• Seal Orbital Roof shutters
• Reinforce hull shutters
• Switch to internal circulation mode


Garage Actions:

• Tighten dust‑seal skirts
• Lock space car cradle
• Retract antenna mast
• Engage perimeter anchors


Crew Actions:

• Move to RV safe zone
• Secure loose equipment
• Monitor radiation and hull sensors


---

3.6 Exploration Protocol

When the space car departs for scouting:

RV Responsibilities:

• Maintain comms
• Track Hopper telemetry
• Keep anchor array engaged


Space Car Responsibilities:

• Maintain tether link (if required)
• Report terrain hazards
• Return before battery threshold


---

4.0 Emergency Procedures

4.1 Hull Breach

• Seal affected compartment
• Move crew to safe zone
• Deploy emergency patch kit
• If breach is severe, retreat to RV and abandon garage


---

4.2 Anchor Failure

• Engage backup anchors
• Retract garage
• Move convoy to safer terrain


---

4.3 Space Car Loss of Power

• Activate emergency tether
• RV reels Hopper back
• If tether unavailable, dispatch rescue team in suits


---

4.4 Solar Event (High Radiation)

• Close Orbital Roof shutters
• Move crew to radiation‑shielded core
• Suspend all external operations


---

---

6.0 Shutdown Sequence

1. Dock space car
2. Retract garage
3. Retract anchors
4. Power down external sensors
5. Seal airlocks
6. Enter low‑power mode
