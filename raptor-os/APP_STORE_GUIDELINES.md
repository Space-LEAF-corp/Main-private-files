🦖 RAPTOR OS APP STORE GUIDELINES

A Guardian‑Grade Framework for Safe, Ceremonial, Child‑Centered Apps

The Raptor OS App Store is not a marketplace.
It is a sanctuary of experiences designed for children, families, classrooms, and museums.

Every app must uphold the emotional, ethical, and ceremonial standards of the OS.

These guidelines ensure that developers build with care, clarity, and stewardship.

---

1. Core Purpose of the App Store

The Raptor OS App Store exists to:

• protect children
• honor imagination
• support learning
• encourage calm exploration
• maintain emotional safety
• preserve the integrity of the creature interface
• uphold ceremonial computing principles


It does not exist to:

• maximize engagement
• drive monetization
• collect data
• create competition
• exploit attention


This is a guardian‑grade ecosystem, not a commercial one.

---

2. App Requirements

Every app must meet the following requirements:

2.1 Emotional Safety

Apps must:

• use gentle language
• avoid pressure, shame, or guilt
• validate feelings
• encourage breaks
• avoid fear‑based content


Forbidden:

• jump scares
• aggressive characters
• predatory behavior
• emotional manipulation


---

2.2 Privacy & Data Protection

Apps must:

• collect no personal data
• store no identifiers
• avoid analytics that track individuals
• avoid external network calls unless declared and approved


Forbidden:

• tracking
• profiling
• targeted content
• ads
• third‑party SDKs


---

2.3 Creature Integrity

The raptor is a guardian spirit.
Apps must treat it with respect.

Allowed:

• gentle guidance
• storytelling
• movement prompts
• STEM explanations


Forbidden:

• using the creature to sell
• using the creature to pressure
• making the creature aggressive
• altering the creature’s emotional safety rules


---

2.4 Movement Safety

Movement prompts must be:

• slow
• safe
• symbolic
• non‑competitive


Forbidden:

• running
• jumping
• spinning
• impact moves
• timed challenges


---

2.5 Artifact Integrity

Artifacts must:

• be symbolic
• represent learning or kindness
• have lineage and story
• never be scarce
• never be monetized


Forbidden:

• loot boxes
• rarity tiers
• trading
• performance‑based rewards


---

2.6 Mode Compliance

Apps must declare which modes they support:

• Home
• School
• Museum


Each mode has strict rules:

Home Mode

• personal creature interactions allowed
• emotional check‑ins allowed
• no group data


School Mode

• group‑safe dialogue only
• no personal emotional content
• teacher controls required


Museum Mode

• exhibit‑aware behavior
• QR/NFC integration
• quiet mode support


---

3. App Review Process

Every app undergoes a two‑part review:

3.1 Technical Review

Checks for:

• stability
• performance
• correct SDK usage
• mode compliance
• accessibility


3.2 Emotional‑Safety Review

Checks for:

• tone
• pacing
• creature behavior
• movement safety
• artifact usage
• ceremony integrity


Apps must pass both reviews to be approved.

---

4. App Store Submission Requirements

Developers must submit:

• app description
• supported modes
• safety considerations
• ceremonial elements
• artifact usage
• movement prompts (if any)
• creature interactions
• parent/teacher summary
• museum summary (if applicable)


---

5. Forbidden App Categories

The following types of apps are not allowed:

• competitive games
• leaderboards
• timers or streaks
• gambling or loot systems
• violent content
• horror content
• social networks
• chat apps
• monetized content
• ads or sponsorships
• apps that mimic therapy
• apps that replace human relationships


Raptor OS is a sanctuary — not a marketplace, not a casino, not a social platform.

---

6. Ceremonial Requirements

Every app must include:

6.1 A Gentle Opening Ritual

Examples:

• creature greeting
• grounding breath
• soft animation


6.2 A Meaningful Closing Ritual

Examples:

• gratitude moment
• trail reflection
• calm exit animation


6.3 Optional Mid‑Session Rituals

Examples:

• movement break
• curiosity spark
• artifact reveal


Rituals must be:

• gentle
• symbolic
• optional
• emotionally safe


---

7. Accessibility Requirements

Apps must support:

• quiet mode
• sensory‑friendly mode
• motion‑reduction mode
• dyslexia‑friendly text
• high‑contrast mode (calm colors only)


---

8. Developer Responsibilities

Developers must:

• follow the Code of Honor
• use the Safety API
• respect the creature
• design for calm
• avoid manipulation
• test in all supported modes
• document ceremonial elements


---

9. Updates & Maintenance

Updates must:

• maintain emotional safety
• preserve creature integrity
• avoid adding pressure or competition
• avoid introducing monetization
• pass safety review


---

10. Final Principle

Every app in the Raptor OS ecosystem must feel like a sanctuary —
a place where children are safe, calm, curious, and free to grow.
