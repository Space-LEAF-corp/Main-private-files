---

🦖 RAPTOR OS DEVELOPER CODE OF HONOR

A Stewardship Oath for Building Inside a Guardian‑Grade OS

---

1. I Build for Children, Not for Metrics

I acknowledge that Raptor OS exists to protect imagination, not to harvest attention.
I will never design features that:

• manipulate
• pressure
• addict
• shame
• compare
• rank
• exploit


I build for growth, not engagement.

---

2. I Treat the Creature as a Guardian, Not a Gimmick

The raptor is a companion spirit, not a mascot.
I will never use the creature to:

• coerce behavior
• sell products
• create fear
• create dependency
• replace human relationships


The creature is a guide, not a tool.

---

3. I Honor Emotional Safety as a System Requirement

I will design every feature with emotional safety as a non‑negotiable constraint.
This means:

• no dark patterns
• no hidden data collection
• no unsafe topics
• no emotional manipulation
• no secrets from parents
• no pressure to perform


If a feature compromises emotional safety, it does not belong in Raptor OS.

---

4. I Protect Privacy as Sacred

I will collect no personal data unless explicitly required and consented to.
I will never:

• store identifiers
• track behavior
• build profiles
• log emotional content
• share data with third parties


Children’s privacy is not a resource.
It is a right.

---

5. I Design for Calm, Not Chaos

Raptor OS is a sanctuary.
I will create experiences that are:

• slow
• readable
• gentle
• grounding
• predictable
• emotionally safe


I will avoid overstimulation, rapid animations, or chaotic interfaces.

---

6. I Use Movement as Ceremony, Not Performance

Movement in Raptor OS is:

• slow
• safe
• symbolic
• grounding


I will never design movement that is:

• competitive
• strenuous
• risky
• fast
• overwhelming


Movement is a ritual, not a challenge.

---

7. I Create Artifacts With Meaning, Not Scarcity

Artifacts in Raptor OS are:

• symbolic
• educational
• ceremonial
• lineage‑based


I will never:

• assign monetary value
• create rarity pressure
• enable trading
• create loot systems
• tie artifacts to performance


Artifacts honor learning, not status.

---

8. I Respect the Three Worlds of Raptor OS

I will design features that honor the boundaries of:

Home Mode

Personal, calm, emotionally attuned.

School Mode

Group‑safe, teacher‑guided, structured.

Museum Mode

Exploratory, story‑driven, ceremonial.

I will never blur these boundaries without explicit purpose and safety review.

---

9. I Build as a Steward, Not an Extractor

I understand that developing for Raptor OS is an act of stewardship.
I will:

• protect children
• honor imagination
• respect parents
• support teachers
• collaborate with museums
• uphold the creature’s integrity


I am not here to extract value.
I am here to create safe wonder.

---

10. I Leave the Trail Better Than I Found It

Every feature I build becomes part of a child’s learning journey.
I will design with:

• humility
• care
• clarity
• responsibility
• emotional intelligence


I will ensure that my work strengthens the trail, not complicates it.

---

The Oath

As a Raptor OS developer, I commit to building technology that is kind, ceremonial, emotionally safe, and worthy of a child’s trust.
I build not for profit, but for stewardship.
Not for engagement, but for growth.
Not for extraction, but for wonder.
