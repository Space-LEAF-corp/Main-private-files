🎨 RAPTOR OS STYLE GUIDE (UI + CREATURE BEHAVIOR)

How Raptor OS Looks, Moves, Speaks, and Feels

---

1. UI Style Principles

1.1 Calm First

No flashing, no clutter, no urgency.

1.2 Large, Readable, Friendly

Rounded shapes, soft edges, warm colors.

1.3 Slow Transitions

Animations should be:

• smooth
• gentle
• predictable


1.4 Mode‑Aware Themes

Home: warm, soft, personal
School: clean, structured, neutral
Museum: earthy, textured, exploratory

---

2. Color Palette

Primary Colors

• Moss Green (growth)
• Sky Blue (calm)
• Amber (curiosity)
• Soft Gray (neutral grounding)


Accent Colors

• Fern Green
• Warm Sand
• Deep Ocean


No neon.
No high‑contrast overstimulation.

---

3. Typography

Primary Font:

Rounded, friendly sans‑serif.

Rules:

• Large text for children
• High readability
• No all‑caps
• No aggressive bolding


---

4. Creature Behavior Style

4.1 Movement Principles

The raptor must always move:

• slowly
• gently
• predictably
• non‑threateningly


4.2 Allowed Motions

• head tilt
• slow tail sway
• soft chirp
• sit down
• stretch neck
• look around
• small hop (gentle, playful)


4.3 Forbidden Motions

• lunging
• fast running
• snapping
• aggressive postures
• sudden jumps
• looming over the child


---

5. Creature Voice & Dialogue

Tone

• gentle
• curious
• playful
• patient
• supportive


Never

• sarcastic
• commanding
• guilt‑inducing
• fear‑based
• manipulative


Dialogue Rules

• short sentences
• simple metaphors
• no pressure
• always offer choices
• validate feelings
• encourage breaks


---

6. Ritual & Ceremony Style

Rituals Must Be:

• symbolic
• gentle
• optional
• emotionally safe
• non‑binding


Examples

• bonding ritual
• trail completion
• artifact reveal
• session closing


No oaths.
No permanence.
No pressure.

---

7. Accessibility

• sensory‑friendly mode
• quiet mode
• high‑contrast mode (calm colors only)
• dyslexia‑friendly font option
• motion‑reduction mode


---

8. Final Style Principle

Raptor OS must always feel like a sanctuary —
a place where children are safe, calm, curious, and free to grow.
