Cheetah‑Speed Risk Factor Calculator (CSR‑FV)

A lightweight, rapid‑assessment framework for evaluating risk under pressure.

Overview

The CSR‑FV is an 8‑factor risk evaluation model designed for fast decision cycles.
It provides a structured way to quantify uncertainty, identify dominant pressures, and generate a clear operational posture in seconds.

The tool is intentionally simple:

• 8 risk factors
• 8 adjustable weights
• 1 cheetah‑loop evaluation pass
• A single composite score (CSR)


This makes it adaptable to technical, operational, creative, and educational environments.

---

Risk Factors (R1–R8)

Each factor uses a 0–10 scale
(0 = none, 10 = extreme).

• R1 — Volatility
• R2 — Resource Exposure
• R3 — Time Pressure
• R4 — Dependency Load
• R5 — Emotional Load
• R6 — System Health (inverse)
• R7 — External Threats
• R8 — Data Confidence (inverse)


These eight dimensions cover both internal and external stressors, allowing the model to surface the true driver of risk.

---

Weights (w1–w8)

Each factor includes a tunable weight (default: 0.5).
Weights allow the model to adapt to different domains:

• engineering
• operations
• project management
• emergency response
• creative pipelines
• education and training


Users can increase or decrease the influence of any factor without changing the underlying structure.

---

Cheetah‑Loop Evaluation

The “cheetah loop” is a fast, single‑pass calculation that outputs:

• CSR (Cheetah‑Speed Risk Score)
• Operational Posture
• Dominant Factor


This loop is designed for clarity and speed — a quick snapshot of the current environment.

---

Why This Exists

CSR‑FV was created as a universal, low‑friction tool for anyone who needs to:

• make decisions under pressure
• compare multiple scenarios
• identify hidden risk drivers
• communicate risk clearly to others
• maintain situational awareness


It is intentionally neutral, modular, and easy to integrate into any workflow.

---

Use Cases

• Rapid project triage

• Personal workload assessment
• Classroom or training simulations
• Operational readiness checks
• Safety‑first decision loops


If someone finds this tool, they can immediately understand it and apply it without needing background context.
